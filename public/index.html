how child-friendly is London, honestly — open data, open methodology

Greater London
Methodology — seven weighted, open-data dimensions. Every input published. Safety 30% (police.uk street-crime density per km², inverted — a deliberate design: dense, high-footfall central wards score lowest, and the worst ~5% of wards all score 0, so a zero means "highest crime density in London", not "no data") · Education 20% · Transport 15% (stations in or adjacent to the ward — stations serve catchments larger than one ward — weighted 3× vs bus stops, per km²) · Green space 12% (parks & playgrounds within the ward — cross-boundary access, e.g. living beside Regent's Park in the next ward over, is v2, same caveat as play) · Family presence 10% (share of households with dependent children — who lives there now, not suitability) · Play provision 8% (OS Open Greenspace playable space per child aged 0–15, scored against the London Plan Policy S4 benchmark of 10 m² per child) · Planning 5% · Composite 0–100, missing dimensions excluded and weights renormalised, never counted as zero. Play measures space within each ward, not walking-distance access (that's v2); 15 of the City of London's 25 wards have no published child population, so play is shown as no data there. All sources open. Disagree with the inputs? Good, they're published.
